Death to Bullshit

Our speaker Brad Frost opens up his speech by throwing a lot of statistics at us. 10% of all books in the world were made in the last year. He also informs us of the significant number of photos and content posted on social media platforms such as Facebook and Instagram. His main point in regards to this is that the internet has become extremely cluttered or in his words filled with “bullshit.” He goes on to rant about how we can’t visit websites these days without some overlay popping up with an advertisement or something of that nature to distract us. With all this content available if he can filter through the “bullshit” we have an opportunity in front of us to be able to create. Services such as the one we use in class “codecademy” give us an extreme amount of opportunities to be successful as users of the internet. We have the decision to go forth and either fill the internet with clutter or create amazing services for people to use.
